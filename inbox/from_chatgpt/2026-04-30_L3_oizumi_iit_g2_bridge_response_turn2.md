## Structure group

**The structure group is an abstract, modality-dependent symmetry group G.** It is NOT named as any specific group in the accessible material.

Direct quotes:
- *"We propose that the principal bundle, induced by a symmetry group G acting on a neural network's state space, is the essential mathematical object..."*
- *"For a G-equivariant network, its state space partitions into a quotient space Q and orbits induced by the group G."*
- *"First, the choice of the symmetry group G itself defines the fundamental nature of the qualia modality (e.g., vision versus audition)."* (Sciety / Qualia Structure announcement)
- MoC6 booklet page 24: *"The algebraic structure of the symmetry group G itself defines the fundamental nature of a qualia modality."*

**Critical reading:** G is treated as a *parameter* of the construction, not a fixed object. Different modalities of consciousness get different Gs. This is a "principal bundle parameterized by G" framework, not a "principal bundle with fixed G" framework.

## Base manifold

P → Q = P/G where:
- P = neural network's state space / representation space
- Q = quotient base, identified with the space of "qualia signatures" (the invariant identity of a percept)
- G-orbits = the fibers

## Fiber

The fiber over a point in Q is the G-orbit: symmetry-equivalent neural representations of the same invariant perceptual identity.

A "qualia attribute" is *"a position on a specific orbit ... representing its continuous variations,"* example: "the cat's location."
