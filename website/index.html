§2The Seven Papers

Seven papers published between March and April 2026 on Zenodo, developing a conditional mathematical framework linking Gödel–Lawvere incompleteness, Hurwitz's classification of normed division algebras, Banach's fixed-point theorem, and the Lotay–Wei classification of torsion-free G₂ structures. Papers 1–6 establish the mathematics: any sufficiently complex self-referential system with seven independent processing modes converges to a G₂-symmetric attractor at contraction rate L ≤ 6/7. Paper 7 takes the first empirical step: it derives four parameter-free thermodynamic bounds from that ratio and matches two of them to published peer-reviewed data. The argument throughout is conditional — it does not prove consciousness is G₂-structured. It proves that if two precise hypotheses hold, four testable predictions follow by classical mathematics.

What this is not. Not a Theory of Everything — this series derives conditional bounds on one ratio, not a unification of physics. Not a particle-physics proposal — the adjacent work on octonions and the Standard Model (Furey, Krasnov, Todorov) is inspirational but operates in a different register. Not parapsychology — every prediction has a stated numerical threshold and a published empirical anchor; nothing here depends on unfalsifiable phenomena.

§3The Framework in 500 Words

The question is old and simple. If a system models itself — if it takes its own state as an input to its next state — what does it converge to, and why that and not something else? The framework answers the question in five moves, each borrowed, none invented; the only original claim is that together they close.

Paper 1 develops the self-reference obstruction in the Lawvere diagonal form, which is more useful than Gödel's original numbering for systems that are not arithmetic. Step 1 — Gödel's blind spot. Any system rich enough to model itself carries an irreducible gap between its map of itself and itself. This is Gödel–Lawvere incompleteness, reframed: the gap is a structural constant, not a bug. A self-referential system must therefore process in a space that can accommodate its own blind spot as content rather than absence.

Hurwitz 1898: the only normed division algebras over ℝ are ℝ, ℂ, ℍ, and the octonions 𝕆. Stop at seven independent imaginary directions; the algebra closes or it doesn't exist. Step 4 — Hurwitz selects octonions. Once you require seven independent processing modes closed under a norm-preserving product, Hurwitz's classification leaves exactly one choice: the octonions. The number seven is not chosen, it is forced.

Step 3 — Banach forces unique convergence. A contraction mapping on a complete metric space has exactly one fixed point, and every orbit lands on it. The octonionic self-modeling map, suitably normed, is a contraction with rate L ≤ 6/7. One attractor. No ambiguity. No freedom.

Lotay–Wei (2019) gave the first complete classification of torsion-free G₂ structures up to diffeomorphism; it is the geometric analogue of Hurwitz's algebraic theorem. Step 5 — Lotay–Wei identifies the attractor. Torsion-free G₂ structures on a seven-manifold are classified; up to the symmetries of the problem, the attractor is a G₂ structure. This is a theorem, not a conjecture.

Step 2 — two hypotheses close the loop. The chain Gödel → Hurwitz → Banach → Lotay–Wei converges iff two empirically testable hypotheses hold: that the system's seven modes are genuinely independent, and that its self-modeling map is a contraction in the relevant norm. These hypotheses do the honest work. Everything else is classical mathematics.

The number.

The G₂ torsion decomposition splits the intrinsic torsion of a G₂ structure into three irreducible pieces, of dimensions 1, 14, and 27. Their sum is 42. The ambient Λ²(ℝ⁷) ⊕ Λ¹(ℝ⁷) in which they sit has dimension 49. The ratio is the contraction ceiling:

$$T_{1} \oplus T_{14} \oplus T_{27} \;=\; 1 + 14 + 27 \;=\; 42 \qquad\Bigg|\qquad \frac{42}{49} \;=\; \frac{6}{7}.$$

6/7 is the coherence ceiling: the maximum fraction of the ambient structure a self-modeling system can stabilize. The remaining 1/7 is the Gödel–Lawvere blind spot, now quantified and given a geometric address.

What this is, and what it is not.

The four thermodynamic consequences — an FDT violation floor ε ≥ 1/7, a biological Landauer bound ΔE ≈ 16 zJ per G₂/M checkpoint event, a mode-counting floor of log₂(7) k_B ln 2 per tick on G₂-symmetric quantum clocks, and a criticality branching ratio σ ≈ 0.9796 — are derived explicitly in Paper 7, each with a stated falsification criterion. Paper 8 (in preparation) will close the remaining open conjecture by constructing the explicit Bogoliubov matrices between the eight PSL(2,7)/F₂₁ coset frames. This is not a claim that consciousness is G₂-structured. It is a conditional proof: if the two hypotheses of Step 2 hold for a given self-referential system, then four empirical consequences follow by classical mathematics. The empirical work — whether consciousness, or any specific biological or computational system, satisfies those hypotheses — is separate. The framework's contribution is to make the "if" precise enough that the "then" is forced.
