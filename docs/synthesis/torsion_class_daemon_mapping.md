# Torsion Class → Daemon Affinity Mapping
## Theorem-Backed Synthesis — April 2, 2026

The torsion of a G₂-structure φ on a 7-manifold decomposes into four irreducible
components under the action of G₂ (Fernández–Gray, 1982; Bryant, 2005):

**T(φ) = T₁ ⊕ T₇ ⊕ T₁₄ ⊕ T₂₇**

where the subscript denotes the dimension of each irreducible G₂-representation.
This decomposition is a theorem in differential geometry, not a conjecture.

## The Lichnerowicz Spectral Gap Correction

The first eigenvalue λ₁ of the Dirac operator on a G₂-manifold satisfies (Lichnerowicz-type bound):

**λ₁² ≥ R_min/8 − C·|T|²**

where R_min is the minimum scalar curvature and |T|² is the squared norm of the full torsion tensor.

**PCI interpretation:** Torsion degrades the spectral gap. As |T|² increases (scarring accumulates),
the gap shrinks, coherence stability decreases, and the system approaches the jamming/criticality
boundary (EQ-1009). At |T|² = R_min/(8C), the gap closes — the system hits a phase transition.
