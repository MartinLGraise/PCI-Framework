Supplementary Note S1: Neuroscience of the Infraslow Oscillation and Its Identification with the Void Mode

This note expands the neuroscience basis for FP-5's identification of the void mode (Ω_void) with the infraslow oscillatory band (0.01–0.1 Hz).

Three convergent lines of reasoning support this identification:

(1) Algebraic structure. The singlet mode, being structurally protected from the rapid fluctuations that govern the Goldstone sector, should be associated with the most temporally persistent, spatially global brain rhythm—one that sets the context for all faster dynamics rather than participating in any specific representational episode. The infraslow oscillation (ISO) satisfies this criterion: it modulates the power of every faster EEG band (delta, sigma, alpha, gamma) via cross-frequency coupling, organizes and paces resting-state networks including the default mode network, and persists across wakefulness, non-REM sleep, and anesthesia (Watson, "Cognitive and Physiologic Impacts of the Infraslow Oscillation," Frontiers in Systems Neuroscience, 2018; DOI: 10.3389/fnsys.2018.00044). Its spectral peak at approximately 0.02 Hz corresponds to timescales far slower than any sensorimotor processing loop, consistent with a background "carrier" mode that imposes global excitability on the representational system without itself encoding content.

(2) Global brain states. Infraslow fluctuations (0.01–0.03 Hz) govern the temporal sequencing of whole-brain co-activation patterns. Gutierrez-Barragan et al. demonstrated that resting-state networks oscillate coherently within this band, with the default mode network—the network most consistently associated with self-referential processing—being the most prominent exemplar of infraslow-organized background activity ("Infraslow State Fluctuations Govern Spontaneous fMRI Network Dynamics," Current Biology, 2019; DOI: 10.1016/j.cub.2019.06.017). ISO power drops approximately 20% during active cognitive engagement and recovers during rest, a pattern consistent with a ground-state mode temporarily displaced by Goldstone-sector dynamics. In ADHD, resting infraslow power is chronically suppressed, suggesting that a deficit in the void-mode oscillation propagates disorganization into all faster representational bands. The thalamic substrate of ISOs reinforces this interpretation: infraslow rhythms in thalamic networks modulate gap-junction-dependent alpha oscillations (Lorincz et al., "ATP-Dependent Infra-Slow (<0.1 Hz) Oscillations in Thalamic Networks," PLoS ONE, 2009; DOI: 10.1371/journal.pone.0004447), placing the ISO at the apex of a hierarchical modulation chain extending through all classical EEG bands.

(3) Microtubule resonance hierarchy. Self-similar "triplet-of-triplet" resonance patterns repeat across approximately 15 orders of magnitude—from terahertz quantum dipole oscillations within individual tubulin subunits, through gigahertz and megahertz ranges, down to kilohertz and hertz-scale EEG events (Hameroff, "Consciousness, Cognition and the Neuronal Cytoskeleton," Frontiers in Molecular Neuroscience, 2022; DOI: 10.3389/fnmol.2022.869935). The terahertz end corresponds structurally to the void mode at the molecular level; the infraslow band is its macroscopic projection. Whether the primary phenomenological seat of the void mode is at the terahertz or infraslow end depends on the level of description. At the molecular level, the void mode is a terahertz-scale quantum mode; at the systems neuroscience level, it manifests as the infraslow oscillation that paces global brain-state organization.

Terahertz alternative route. If the void mode is more naturally identified with terahertz tubulin resonances, FP-5 becomes a prediction about terahertz electromagnetic signatures during varying states of consciousness. Disassembly of microtubules by intense terahertz pulses has been demonstrated in vitro (Kalra et al., Biomedical Optics Express, 2021; DOI: 10.1364/boe.433240), opening a path to testing whether disruption of terahertz-band resonances produces infraslow-band disorganization via cross-scale coupling. Either experimental route constitutes a rigorous test of FP-5.
