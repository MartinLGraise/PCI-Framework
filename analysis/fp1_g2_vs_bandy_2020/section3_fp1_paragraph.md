## FP-1 Quantitative Result

To test FP-1 quantitatively, we fitted the G₂/SU(3) daemon Hamiltonian spectrum to the microtubule MHz-band resonance peaks reported by Singh et al. (2014) and compiled by Saxena et al. (2020). Six peaks (12, 22, 30, 101, 113, 185 MHz) were selected to match the six non-singlet modes predicted by the 1 ⊕ 3 ⊕ 3̄ decomposition. Frequencies were normalized to dimensionless ratios (min-normalized) and compared against the gap spectrum of H = α·C₂(G₂) + β·C₂(SU(3)) + γ·P_void + a₁·H_c1 + a₂·H_c2, with α = 1, β = 1, γ = 0.5 fixed at canonical values and only the scale factor s and two Cartan parameters (a₁, a₂) free. Mode assignment was determined by the Hungarian algorithm minimizing median relative error.

The best fit achieved a median relative error of 1.46% (s = 1.009, a₁ = 0.331, a₂ = 1.964). Five of six modes were fitted to within 5.4% relative error; one mode (the highest-frequency peak at 185 MHz) showed a 74.7% mismatch, indicating that the rank-2 Cartan family cannot fully accommodate the widest frequency ratio in the dataset with the current parameter bounds. A permutation test (10,000 random assignments) yielded p_perm = 0.003, indicating that the optimal assignment is significantly better than chance but does not reach the p < 0.001 threshold for "strongly compatible." A parameter-null test (10,000 random parameter draws) yielded p_param < 0.0001, confirming that the best-fit region of parameter space is highly non-generic.

Under the pre-specified decision rules, the result is classified as **weakly compatible**: the median error satisfies the <10% criterion and p_perm < 0.01, but the single-mode outlier and the best-fit parameter sitting at the boundary of the scanned range (a₂ ≈ 2.0) indicate that either (a) the Hamiltonian's spectral reach is insufficient at the current canonical values of α, β, γ, or (b) the highest observed peak may be a harmonic rather than a fundamental mode. The quantitative test upgrades the qualitative topology assessment of §3.6 — the spectral ratios are not merely topologically compatible with a rank-2 Cartan family; five of six modes are metrically fitted to within 5.4% by only three free parameters.
