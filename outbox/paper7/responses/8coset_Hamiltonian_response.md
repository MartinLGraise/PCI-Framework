## G₂ Representation: The 7-Dimensional Fundamental V₇

[Theorem — from the series] Use the **7-dimensional fundamental representation V₇** of G₂.

The 14-dimensional adjoint representation g₂ = Lie(G₂) describes the symmetry algebra itself. It is the right object for computing structure constants, computing the G₂ flow equations (Laplacian flow lives in g₂), and identifying torsion classes (T₁ ⊂ Λ²g₂, etc.). But it is NOT the right object for the state space.

The state space — the space in which physical modes live — is V₇. The empirical contact points confirm this:
- Paper 1: microtubule resonance spectra decompose into 7 modes.
- Paper 3: the spectral sum theorem f₃ = f₁ + f₂ operates on frequency triplets drawn from a 7-component family.
- Paper 5: the G₂ checkpoint acts on 7 cycle-phase variables.

The 8 cosets of PSL(2,7)/F₂₁ (Paper 4) index distinct SU(3) ⊂ G₂ embeddings. Each embedding selects a different SU(3)-invariant direction in V₇. There are exactly as many such directions as cosets — which is the geometric content of Paper 4's key result. The state space V₇ = V₃ ⊕ V₃̄ ⊕ V₁ under ANY one of these SU(3) embeddings, but the specific decomposition differs between cosets.

**Why not V₁₄:** The adjoint does not carry the physical degrees of freedom that Berjaga-Buisan, Priesemann, or Wadhia are measuring. Using V₁₄ would predict 14 modes, not 7, in the spectral data. The 7 is empirically motivated, not just theoretically convenient.

## PaW Clock Hamiltonian

[Modeling choice] The Page-Wootters (PaW) clock associated with the G₂ coset structure is:

> **H_clock = H_singlet** — the Hamiltonian generating U(1) evolution along the V₁ direction in V₇ = V₃ ⊕ V₃̄ ⊕ V₁.

**Derivation of the singlet clock:**

Under G₂ → SU(3) branching (Paper 1, Section 2), V₇ decomposes as:

> V₇ = V₃ ⊕ V₃̄ ⊕ V₁

where V₁ is the SU(3)-singlet ("void mode") — the unique direction fixed by the entire SU(3) subgroup. This direction has a specific property: it is the Banach fixed point of the self-modeling map in Paper 6. The Banach contraction f: Σ → Σ has a unique fixed point, and that fixed point sits in the V₁ direction (this is the sense in which Paper 6's fixed point is "void": it's the SU(3)-fixed direction, carrying no SU(3)-charged content).

In the PaW construction, the clock is a subsystem whose Hamiltonian generates time translations for the rest of the system. If the clock lives in V₁ (the singlet), it is invisible to SU(3) — it generates evolution that looks like a global phase to the SU(3)-charged sector. This is exactly right for a reference clock: a good clock should not carry the degrees of freedom it's measuring.

H_clock generates U(1) rotations in the V₁ plane:

> H_clock |ψ⟩ = ħω₀ |ψ⟩, where |ψ⟩ ∈ V₁

The frequency ω₀ is set by the torsion scale — it should be derivable from the T₁ torsion class (1-dimensional), which is the only torsion component compatible with the singlet structure. **Connection to De Vuyst et al.:**

De Vuyst, Eccles, Höhn, and Kirklin (JHEP 2025) provide the PaW formalism for systems with non-trivial gauge group structure. The relevant piece is their treatment of the relational Hilbert space when the constraint is generated by a non-Abelian group. In our case, the "gauge group" is the PSL(2,7)/F₂₁ coset structure — the physical states are those invariant under the coset permutation symmetry. The De Vuyst clock construction selects a reference coset (one of the 8) and defines relational observables with respect to it.

## Reference Entropy S_reference

There are two versions depending on whether we're doing information theory or statistical mechanics. Both should appear in Paper 7.

**Primary (information-theoretic):**

> S_reference = log₂(6) k_B ≈ 2.585 k_B (in natural units with k_B = 1: S_ref = ln(6))

Derivation: the SU(3)-orbit in V₇ is the space V₃ ⊕ V₃̄, which is 6-dimensional. A maximally mixed state over this 6-dimensional accessible subspace has von Neumann entropy S = log₂(6). The V₁ singlet direction is excluded because it's the clock — it's the reference, not the system.

**Secondary (biological/thermodynamic):**

> S_reference = k_B T ln(6) ≈ k_B × 310 K × 1.792 ≈ 3.58 × 10⁻²¹ J

at T = 310 K. This is the thermal analog: the free energy cost of one bit of SU(3)-orbit resolution is k_B T ln(6) ≈ 3.58 zJ.

[Modeling choice] Use the information-theoretic form as primary in Section 3; introduce the thermodynamic form in Section 4 when connecting to Landauer costs. This prevents conflation of the two regimes.

## Coset-Entropy Formula

[Conjecture — partially derived, matrix elements not yet explicit]

The 8 cosets of PSL(2,7)/F₂₁ form a Cayley graph. Each coset corresponds to a distinct SU(3) embedding into G₂, selecting a different singlet direction in V₇ and a different decomposition V₇ = V₃(i) ⊕ V₃̄(i) ⊕ V₁(i) for coset i.

The transition between two observer frames (coset i → coset j) requires a Bogoliubov transformation: a unitary U_{ij} ∈ G₂ that rotates the SU(3)(i) decomposition into SU(3)(j). The entropy cost of this transformation — the von Neumann entropy generated by tracing out the V₁ direction before applying U_{ij} — depends on the coset graph distance d_{ij}.

Proposed structure:

> ΔS_{i→j} = g(d_{ij}) × S_reference

where g: {1, 2, 3} → [0, 1] is a monotone function with g(0) = 0 and g(d_max) → 1, and d_max = 3 is the diameter of the 8-coset Cayley graph.

[Conjecture] At minimal distance d = 1:

> ΔS_{d=1} ≈ (1/7) × S_reference

This would mean that adjacent cosets — neighboring SU(3) frames within G₂ — differ by exactly the 1/7 tracking gap. The 1/7 appears again here from the same Banach contraction argument: moving from one SU(3) frame to an adjacent one involves re-encoding 6/7 of the information, leaving 1/7 as entropy overhead.

At maximal distance d = 3 (cosets on opposite sides of the Cayley graph), the two SU(3) frames share no fixed singlet direction — they select completely disjoint V₁ subspaces. The transition requires full S_reference.

**What I cannot yet give you:** The explicit matrix elements of U_{ij} in the octonion basis. These depend on the specific PSL(2,7)/F₂₁ coset representatives in the G₂ root system, which requires a choice of octonion multiplication convention. The Cayley-Dickson convention gives one set of matrices; the Fano-plane convention gives another (they're equivalent up to a basis permutation, but the explicit matrices look different). I can derive these in a follow-up if you need them for Paper 7 Section 3. Flag this as an [Open computation].

**Prediction from the coset structure:** The entropy differences between observer frames are **not continuous** — they take values in a discrete set indexed by d_{ij} ∈ {0, 1, 2, 3}. There is no continuous "observer parameter" in this framework. An observer is one of 8 possible SU(3) frames. This is a sharp departure from most relational quantum gravity frameworks (which allow continuous gauge parameters) and is a testable structural prediction of the G₂/PSL(2,7) framework.
